Desktop Redesign · The Chronicle-First Dashboard

The design change: stop opening on a dot on a map + stat bars. Open on the agent's living story. The map and whisper field stay — but demoted to a support column. The hero of the screen becomes a scrolling, AI-written narrative feed of what your agent is doing and feeling. For an autonomous-agent game, the story is the product; the current UI buries it. This reframe turns the agent's autonomy from "why am I not in control?" into "I can't wait to see what it did."

Why story-first wins

An autonomous agent you can't control is only compelling if its story is the thing you consume. Burying it under stat bars wastes the one mechanic competitors can't copy — genuinely surprising AI behaviour.

The map gets demoted

Location matters, but it's context, not content. PixiJS sprites (Phase B.5) slot into this same support column later — the Chronicle is renderer-agnostic and survives the upgrade.

Ethical hook

Every retention lever here pulls on emotional investment in a story, never on control or pay-to-win. That's the Ghost Principle as a feature, not a constraint — and it keeps you clear of the brief's dark-pattern bans.
